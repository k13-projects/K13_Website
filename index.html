Tiger Hospitality

Brand site · Interactive maps · Motion · Accessibility

A parent brand has one job: make every child look like the favorite. I gave this family one address that raises all its names at once.

Velvet black and gold on purpose. A house brand should be the case, not the loudest jewel inside it. The heart is an interactive map where each location opens with its concepts' logos side by side, because this group's real story is geography. Every pin is a bet placed on a neighborhood.

Global Fork

Next.js · Lenis · scroll choreography

This one came with a standard to meet. The venue was designed by Paul Basile of Basile Studio, and the site had to feel like it belonged in that world.

Global Fork is a food hall in San Diego's Little Italy: a 4,685 sq ft hall opening onto a 10,000 sq ft cobblestone plaza, five concepts around a central bar. I studied Basile's work and built the site to honor it, warm sandstone and clay, a single-page flow paced like a stroll through a piazza.

The signature moment

The vendor piazza walk, a scroll sequence that turns a list of tenants into a place you move through. The kind of detail you only build when you care more than the brief asks.

Station8

Next.js · Sanity CMS · Mapbox · accessibility-first

A 20,000 sq ft public market at UC San Diego is going to get a lot of traffic. So the brief was simple: launch clean, and don't hand the client a problem six months later.

We did the research. We looked at where sites like this go wrong for other people, the broken handoffs, the accessibility complaints, and built to avoid all of it. 508 and WCAG accessibility is baked in and tested in CI, the team owns their content through a CMS, and the site is fast and stable from day one.

Why it matters

An inaccessible public site can mean real legal exposure. We deliver one that won't put the client in that position. That peace of mind is part of the product.

Next.js · Motion · Lenis · Resend

Baha works in Roman clay and limewash, the kind of surfaces you want to touch. His site had to whisper, and a whisper is the hardest voice to engineer.
