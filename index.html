Edisyn
Role — Product · Engineering · Design Status — Live, shipping nightly Year — 2025 → now
React + TSSupabaseDeepgram 5 LLM providersStripeDesktop app
The palette
edisyn.ai ↗

A meeting is the worst place to think. Edisyn listens with you — and hands you the answer while the question is still hanging in the air.

It started as a transcription panel. It became a product: live speech-to-text with speaker separation, an AI copilot that detects questions and drafts responses in real time, multi-profile contexts, summaries, billing, an admin panel, a prompt lab for testing the AI against mock meetings — then a desktop app.

The router behind it speaks to five AI providers and picks per request, by cost and latency. The night Stripe said "paid" while the app said "free tier" is the night we learned to love webhooks. The fix shipped. So did the nightly Playwright suite that now smoke-tests production while we sleep.

A restaurant group is a family of brands. The brief: give the family one address — and make every member look like the favorite child.

Tiger Hospitality runs culinary concepts and food halls across Southern California. We built the group a black-and-gold home with a loading screen that reveals the tiger, an interactive map where each location pops up with its brands' logos side by side, and a vendor CTA that flips over to reveal the email — a tiny moment people actually remember.

Twenty-one release branches later it's the group's front door: concepts, journey, careers, vendor opportunities — and a map pin for every opening, most recently Oceanside.

Miramar Food Hall
Role — Site · Brand system · Reporting Status — Live · venue opening Year — 2025 → 2026
HTML/CSS/JSGitHub ActionsJosefin Sans
The palette
Live site ↗

In 1938 it showed films. In 2026 it serves the world on a plate. The Miramar Theatre in San Clemente — restored as a food hall, and we got to build its digital marquee.

The brand says “Global Bites, Local Vibes” and the site says it in teal, cream and burnt orange: vendor cards with Instagram links aligned to the pixel, a footer with wave dividers, and an animated Coming Soon stamp slammed across the hero while the kitchen crews move in.

Then the part clients rarely get: every month, stakeholders receive a progress report built as a single HTML file — images embedded, Gmail-safe, forwardable to anyone. The site got a security-hardening pass before the first stakeholder ever clicked it.

Station8 Public Market
Role — Next-gen site · CMS · Maps Status — Pre-launch hardened Year — 2026
Next.js 15Tailwind v4GSAP Sanity CMSMapbox GLPlaywright + axe
The palette

Twenty thousand square feet of food hall at UC San Diego's Theater District. The site had to feel like the building: industrial bones, warm light, everything labeled.

Built like a product, not a brochure: Sanity CMS so the team edits vendors, events and copy without touching code; a custom Mapbox style mixed to the brand's olive and green-glass; GSAP motion on an accessibility budget — the whole thing is 508-compliant and axe-tested in CI.

Favorite war story: the homepage went silent one day — a Content-Security-Policy header was quietly killing every animation. The commit that fixed it is titled, honestly, “Unbreak the homepage.”

V
TrustMeBro
Role — Product · Odds engine · UI Status — Private beta Year — 2026
Next.jsTypeScriptEV engineLive scoring
The palette

Every group chat has the friend who says “trust me, bro.” We gave him an expected-value engine and made him show his math.

TrustMeBro generates picks with live odds, scores them transparently on the Bro Board, and tracks every coupon leg by leg. Forty-eight pull requests of probability, edge cases and UI honesty — including the alt-line engine waiting on a live June smoke-test.

The detail I'm proudest of is invisible until it matters: when a game gets cancelled mid-coupon, the engine doesn't lazily refund your whole ticket. It re-prices the remaining legs — exactly like the real sportsbooks do. Correctness is a feature.

Baha Irs paints surfaces the way other people write memoirs. Her atelier needed a site that whispers — Playfair italics, paper tones, sage and clay.

We built it like a magazine: masked headline reveals, momentum scroll, project spreads, a collection section, and an inquiry form that delivers straight to her inbox. Editorial restraint is harder than maximalism — every pixel had to earn its quiet.

And because beauty without business is just decoration, the most honest commit in the whole repo is the one that says: “Make the site sell, not just show.” That sentence is now this studio's quality bar.
